Fasteners Intelligence System

Why FIS Exists

In safety-critical industries, failures rarely originate from the fastener alone.
They arise from unvalidated assumptions.

Hidden Engineering Assumptions

Preload, friction, stiffness, fatigue behaviour, and joint compliance are often assumed — not validated — during fastener selection.

Compliance ≠ Validation

Meeting a manufacturing standard does not guarantee joint integrity under real service conditions.

Failure Happens Systemically

Most failures occur due to system-level gaps — not material defects alone.

With FIS, design becomes compliance.
Without validation, design is intent.

FIS exists to eliminate assumption-driven risk by enforcing engineering responsibility for bolted joint validation — before failure can occur in service.

Standards Intelligence Inside FIS


It aligns, cross-references, and connects them into one validated engineering framework.

VDI 2230

Core bolted joint calculation and validation, including preload determination, joint stiffness, fatigue safety, and failure mode analysis.

NASA-STD-5020

Aerospace-grade bolted joint integrity requirements addressing margin of safety, verification rigor, and mission-critical reliability.

ASME PCC-1 / ASME Section VIII

Flanged joint assembly, pressure boundary integrity, gasket behavior, and bolting practices for high-risk systems.

ISO / EN Standards

International alignment for fatigue behavior, preload consistency, thread geometry, tolerances, and material compliance.

JSME / SAE Aerospace Standards

Regional and program-specific aerospace requirements integrated into FIS to ensure global OEM acceptance and audit readiness.
